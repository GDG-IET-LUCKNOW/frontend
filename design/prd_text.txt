1 Project Overview
The goal is to build a modern, futuristic, and community-focused website for GDG on Campus
IET. The website should showcase events, team members, community activities, and attract
new students to join.
The design language should feel:
•Futuristic
•Dark-themed
•Interactive
•Slightly 3D
•Smooth and premium
•Inspired by developer tools, boot sequences, holograms, and modern tech products
The website should not feel like a typical college club page.
2 Goals
•Increase awareness about GDG on Campus IET
•Showcase upcoming and past events
•Highlight the core team and chapter achievements
•Allow students to join the community easily
•Build a memorable first impression
3 Target Audience
•Students interested in development
•Beginners exploring tech communities
•Designers and content creators
•Potential sponsors and collaborators
•Speakers and mentors
4 Suggested Tech Stack
Keep the stack minimal and avoid overengineering.
3
4.1 Frontend
•Next.js
•Tailwind CSS
•Framer Motion
•GSAP ScrollTrigger
•Lenis for smooth scrolling
•Spline for hero 3D scene
4.2 Backend
•No heavy backend initially
•Use static JSON files or local data for events and team members
•Optional: Google Forms or Formspree for contact/join forms
4.3 Deployment
•Vercel for deployment
•GitHub for version control
5 Pages and Detailed Requirements
6 Home Page
6.1 Hero Section
The hero section should immediately create a futuristic impression.
Content Suggestions:
•Heading: Build. Learn. Connect.
•Subheading: GDG on Campus IET is a community of developers, designers, and innova-
tors.
•Buttons:
–Join Community
–Explore Events
Animation Ideas:
•Boot sequence animation before hero appears
•Floating 3D laptop or holographic screen
•Floating cubes for AI, Web, Android, Cloud
•Mouse-follow movement for hero scene
Prompt for 3D Hero Generation:
Create a futuristic dark-themed 3D scene with a floating holographic laptop, glowing
cubes, orbit rings, neon grid floor, developer icons, and blue-purple lighting.

•Workshops Conducted

13 3D Elements and Placement Guide
13.1 Hero Section
The hero section should contain the strongest 3D visuals because it creates the first impression.
Recommended Elements:
•Floating holographic laptop in the center
•Orbiting cubes with icons for AI, Android, Web, Cloud, and Design
•Neon grid floor in the background
•Floating particles and small glowing spheres
•Rotating wireframe globe to represent community
•Transparent holographic UI panels floating around the main object
Placement:
•Laptop in the center-right of the hero section
•Text content on the left side
•Floating cubes around the laptop scene
•Globe placed in the far background with slow rotation
•Neon grid floor at the bottom of the hero scene
9
13.2 About Page
Recommended Elements:
•Rotating ring structure behind the mission section
•Floating icon chips for networking, learning, hackathons, and mentorship
•Small orbit animation around milestone nodes in the timeline
Placement:
•Ring structure behind the main heading
•Floating chips around the mission cards
•Timeline nodes connected with glowing lines and small animated dots
13.3 Domains Section
Recommended Elements:
•Central glowing sphere with orbiting domain icons
•Floating mini cards for AI/ML, Web, Android, Cloud, UI/UX, Open Source
•Rotating rings around the center sphere
Placement:
•Sphere in the center of the section
•Domain cards arranged in circular layout around the sphere
•Each domain card zooms slightly on hover
13.4 Team Page
Recommended Elements:
•Glassmorphism profile cards
•Subtle hover tilt effect on team cards
•Floating holographic borders around selected team leads
•Animated particles behind the cards
Placement:
•Team cards arranged in grid format
•Important members like Lead and Co-Lead displayed larger
•Small floating particles in the section background
10
13.5 Events Page
Recommended Elements:
•Glowing event timeline path
•Floating event posters with layered depth effect
•Small animated location pins and calendar icons
•Hover effect with event cards lifting slightly upward
Placement:
•Timeline path vertically centered
•Event cards alternating left and right
•Featured event poster larger than other cards
13.6 Gallery Page
Recommended Elements:
•Floating photo cards with depth effect
•Slight 3D rotation on hover
•Stacked polaroid style layout
•Soft glowing shadows under images
Placement:
•Images arranged in masonry layout
•Larger images placed in center for visual balance
•Hovered image comes slightly forward compared to others
13.7 Join Us Page
Recommended Elements:
•Large glowing ring or portal behind the CTA
•Floating particles around the join button
•Animated line connections between benefits cards
Placement:
•Portal placed behind the final CTA text
•Join form on the left side
•Benefits cards on the right side with animated connectors
11
13.8 Footer
Recommended Elements:
•Small floating icons for social platforms
•Thin glowing divider line
•Minimal particle effect in background
Placement:
•Social icons centered
•Divider line above footer links
•Particle effect spread lightly across the footer background
14 Recommended Animations
•Smooth scrolling using Lenis
•Fade-in section reveal on scroll
•Cards entering from left and right
•Parallax movement in hero section
•Floating particles in background
•Mouse-follow glow effects
•Text reveal animation
•Rotating 3D objects in hero
•Glowing timeline path for events
15 Performance Guidelines
•Keep Spline scenes lightweight
•Avoid too many heavy 3D objects
•Compress images before upload
•Use lazy loading for gallery images
•Keep animation duration smooth and short
16 Final Notes
The website should feel premium but still remain fast and simple to maintain.
Focus more on storytelling, strong visuals, and community feeling rather than adding too
many unnecessary features.
